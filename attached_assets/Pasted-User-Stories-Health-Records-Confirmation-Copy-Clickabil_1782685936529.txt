User Stories: Health Records Confirmation Copy, Clickability Indicators & Opportunities Design

HEALTH RECORDS — CONFIRMATION PAGE COPY

Story 1 — Update Messaging on Institution Confirmation Page
As a user who has selected a hospital or health system during the Add Health System flow,

I want the confirmation screen to display the exact messaging shown in the attached screenshot,

So that the language is clear, on-brand, and accurately reflects what I am agreeing to.
Acceptance Criteria:

The current text "Do you have an account?" and any surrounding copy on the institution confirmation screen is completely replaced with the messaging shown in the attached screenshot.
The new copy must be reproduced verbatim — no rewording, paraphrasing, or reformatting.
The layout, font size, and position of the new messaging must match the attached screenshot exactly.

This update applies to the confirmation screen reached via: Connect Health Records → Add Health System → Search by Hospital, Clinic, Health System or Pharmacy → Select Institution → Confirmation Screen.


HEALTH RECORDS HOME — CLICKABILITY INDICATORS

Story 2 — Make Immunizations, Visits & Lab Results Visually Clickable
As a user on the Health Records home screen,

I want the Immunizations, Visits, and Lab Results items to look clearly interactive and tappable,

So that I immediately know I can tap them to view more detail without guessing.
Acceptance Criteria:

All three items — Immunizations, Visits, and Lab Results — must be styled to communicate interactivity using the following design treatment:

Color: Apply a green accent color to each card or row (border, icon, or label) to signal that it is active and tappable.
Affordance indicator: Add a right-facing chevron arrow ( › ) on the right side of each item, which is a universally understood "tap to go deeper" pattern in mobile design.
Touch state: Each item must have a visible pressed/highlighted state when tapped (e.g. background darkens slightly or scales down) to confirm the tap registered.
Elevation or border: Apply a subtle card shadow or green-tinted border around each item to lift it visually off the page and distinguish it as interactive.


The three items must be visually consistent with each other — same color treatment, same chevron, same card style.
Static/non-tappable elements on the same screen must not receive this treatment, so the contrast between tappable and non-tappable is clear.
The solution must follow platform conventions — no custom gestures or non-standard patterns.


OPPORTUNITIES PAGE — STRUCTURE & CARD DESIGN

Story 3 — Place Mail Delivery Opportunities Under Care Site Opportunities
As a user browsing the Opportunities page,

I want Mail Delivery Opportunities to appear as a sub-category or grouped item under Care Site Opportunities,

So that the information architecture is logical and related opportunities are grouped together.
Acceptance Criteria:

On the Opportunities page, Mail Delivery Opportunities is nested under or grouped beneath the Care Site Opportunities section — it is not a standalone top-level category.
The grouping is visually clear — Care Site Opportunities acts as the parent, and Mail Delivery Opportunities sits beneath it as a child item or sub-section.
When the Care Site Alternative filter category is selected, Mail Delivery Opportunities entries appear within those results.
The section heading or label hierarchy must make the parent-child relationship obvious to the user at a glance.


Story 4 — Every Opportunity Card Matches the Medication Opportunity Design
As a user viewing any opportunity on the Opportunities page,

I want every opportunity card to look exactly like the Medication Opportunity card design,

So that the page is visually consistent and every card follows the same layout and style.
Acceptance Criteria:

The Medication Opportunity card is the single design template for all opportunity cards across the entire Opportunities page.
Every other opportunity card — including all Care Site Opportunities, Mail Delivery Opportunities, and any other category — must replicate the Medication Opportunity card exactly: layout, spacing, font sizes, font weights, colors, button style, icon/logo placement, and any labels or tags.
The only differences allowed between cards are:

The name/title of the opportunity (unique per card).
The logo or brand icon of the relevant provider or institution (unique per card).


No other visual differences are permitted — background color, card shape, padding, typography, and button design must be identical across all cards.
If a card previously had a different design, it must be updated to match the Medication Opportunity template.
